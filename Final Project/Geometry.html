Area - size of a surface

Square and Rectangle

The area of a rectangle or square is its length and hight multiplied.


Because squares are equal on all sides their area is the length of a side squared


A = length × width

Triangle

The surface area of a triangle is the length of the bottom of the triangle times the height and divided by 2. The height is found from the same side as the base to the highest point across from the base.


A = (base × hight) / 2

Circle

The area of a circle is Π (3.14) times the length of the radius squared. The radius is the length from the center of the circle to the outside.
